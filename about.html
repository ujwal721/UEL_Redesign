About UEL

Urban health is not an abstract.

It is shaped by the neighborhoods people move through, the systems that govern access and risk, and the evidence used to make policy.

The Urban Epidemiology Lab focuses on health outcomes among urban dwellers. Its work spans substance use and infectious diseases, including HIV, hepatitis B and C, sexually transmitted infections, and malaria.

UEL has included undergraduate students, graduate students, and post-doctoral fellows from NYU and beyond. The lab’s perspective centers health equity, the needs of marginalized communities, and approaches that reduce harm rather than deepen stigma.

Our approach

  • Center lived experience
  • Study urban environments in context
  • Translate evidence for practice and policy
  • Build rigorous, student-engaged research

How we work

Multiple methods.
One public-health purpose.

01

Field research

Documenting retail environments, community contexts, and consumer-facing health information where people encounter it.

02

Population analysis

Using epidemiologic and statistical methods to study health patterns, disparities, risk, and protective factors.

03

Community-informed inquiry

Pairing data with qualitative and harm-reduction perspectives to surface the realities behind health outcomes.

“The city is both the setting and the subject.”

UEL’s work asks how place, policy, products, systems, and social conditions shape health for people living in urban environments.
