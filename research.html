UEL’s current work connects package-data quality, field observation, and GIS mapping to better understand cannabis and hemp retail environments across New York City.

01

Data quality & harmonization

Cannabis package data cleaning

This project builds a clean, analysis-ready dataset from cannabis package records collected through multiple data-collection tools. The workflow standardizes package identifiers, reconciles repeated records, harmonizes variable formats, and documents corrections so the resulting data are reliable and reproducible.

Quality-assurance tables and manual-review flags are used to resolve ambiguous package IDs, inconsistent text entries, and differences across survey instruments before analysis begins.

02

GIS mapping paper

Mapping hemp stores across NYC

This GIS-focused project maps hemp-store locations across New York City using ArcGIS. It organizes and validates store-location data, visualizes the retail landscape, and examines how stores are distributed across neighborhoods, ZIP codes, and boroughs.

The resulting maps and spatial summaries will support a paper describing the geographic pattern of hemp retail access across the city.

03

Field survey & data collection

Cannabis & hemp store field survey

This project conducts structured field-based data collection in cannabis and hemp stores. Using a standardized survey protocol, the team documents retail contexts, product and package characteristics, and in-store observations that shape what consumers encounter.

The field survey produces an organized dataset that can be linked with package information and store-location data for future analysis of cannabis and hemp retail environments.
